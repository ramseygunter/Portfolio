Case Study · UX Design · Branding

Charvick Farm

Role
UX Designer
Duration
2 months
Team
Charvick Farm Executives

Project overview

Brief

Charvick Farm is a family-owned, small business that specializes in selling ethically harvested wood products. As the sole UX Designer, I managed this project from inception to final design.

Problem

Charvick Farm has no online presence, which makes it difficult for customers to purchase their products without going to the farm in person.

Solution

An e-commerce website that allows customers to easily and securely browse and purchase products directly from Charvick Farm.

Understanding the user

User research

I conducted exploratory research on common online shopping pain points, then interviewed Charvick Farm customers to understand their needs, behaviors, and motivations.

Exploratory research on online shopping pain points

8 interviewees with varying technical expertise

12 questions about the online shopping experience

Feedback and findings

"I look for secure payment options and the ability to use Apple Pay."

— User

"I do not like complicated online stores. I do not want to have to go searching for what I want under layers of unimportant information."

— User
Personas

From the feedback and findings, I created personas that reflected the target users.

Pain points
1
Slow checkout process
6 of 8 interviewees stated that a slow checkout process made them less inclined to complete the purchase
2
Missing product information
4 of 8 interviewees stated that product information has been hard to find or nonexistent
3
Limited payment options
People prefer to have various payment options (eg. credit card, PayPal, Apple Pay, etc.)
4
No filtering option
8 of 8 interviewees stated that they were frustrated by the lack of filtering options
5
Complicated navigation
Complicated navigation frustrates users and leads to a poor user experience

Understanding the business

Business goals

After analyzing the user research, I sat down with the executives of Charvick Farm to align on business goals. I wanted to make sure that the website not only reflected user needs, but also the company's objectives.

1
Easily manageable online store
2
Increase online presence
3
Increase revenue
4
Promote sustainability
5
Supplement retirement income

Crafting the brand

Brainstorming

After addressing business goals, we began brainstorming about the Charvick Farm brand, as the current brand identity was minimal. I wanted to ensure the website retained the values and meaning that Charvick Farm held in the hearts and minds of the founders, so I asked them to list any words, phrases, or colors they associated with Charvick Farm and identify 5 to 6 keywords they felt embodied the company. I used this information as the design and branding inspiration moving forward.

Choosing the colors

I combined the color preferences provided by the executives with color psychology to select the best colors for the Charvick Farm brand. I incorporated many natural and earthy colors that users would expect from a farm, along with neutral colors that resembled the colors found in wood. Color combinations were also evaluated to ensure they met the WCAG 2.0 guidelines for accessibility.

Designing the logo

We used Crazy 8s to ideate logos and decided that the iconic Charvick Farm sign should serve as the logo. Then, I began digitizing the sign.

Starting the design

User journey map

I mapped out the user flow to gain an understanding of the steps a user would take to browse products and check out. This also helped to identify what screens to include in the design.

Site map

Then, I created a sitemap and organized the information in a way that would be most intuitive to users while promoting business goals.

Paper wireframes

Afterward, I put pen to paper and began to design wireframes. I made various iterations of the desktop version of the homepage to determine which layout would best suit users at the beginning of their user journey. Then, I sketched the main user flow of finding and purchasing a product, including a few variations of the Cart screen.

Digital wireframes

After reviewing the paper wireframes with the Charvick Farm executives, I transferred the most promising features to digital wireframes. I included both desktop and mobile versions to ensure the designs were responsive to a variety of screen sizes.

Prototyping

I merged the branding into the designs and created a high fidelity prototype of the shopping flow on both desktop and mobile.

Adding charisma

Voice and tone

I created additional content to enhance the user experience with Charvick Farm and to provide a family-owned, small-business feel. I used title case throughout the designs to give it an old-fashioned flair while using word choice and flow to reflect the fun and relaxed style of the brand. I also emphasized their concern and love for sustainability and transparency, and highlighted the family members who help run the business to encourage a welcoming, "at home" feel for customers.

Testing the design

Usability testing

After creating the high fidelity prototype, I conducted usability testing with users who had varying degrees of technical expertise and experience with online shopping. This study offered insights into the parts of the design that were unclear or difficult for the user.

5 online shoppers with varying technical expertise

5 questions about the prototype

?

Open ended question about the experience

Insights
1
There is no 'save for later' option when viewing the cart
2
Users would like to see the cart without leaving the current page
3
'Shipping' and 'Billing' labels lack clarity during check out

Refining the design

Usability changes
  • The cart was changed to be an overlay instead of a separate page to allow users to easily view it without losing their place.
  • A "Save for later" button was also added to provide users with more flexibility when shopping.
  • Wording was made clearer to prevent confusion during the checkout process. Changes included renaming "Shipping" to "Shipping Details" and "Billing" to "Billing Address."

Final designs

Mockups

I finalized the mockups with Charvick Farm product photos to ensure that the website design complemented their products too.

Next steps

  • Conduct additional research and study metrics to see if any areas need iteration
  • Add content and graphics that reinforce sustainability and Charvick Farm's family-owned roots

Conclusion

Takeaways

This project taught me a lot about collaborating directly with executives and designing with sustainability in mind. I look forward to using this knowledge and experience in future projects.
