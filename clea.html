CLEA

Role
Content Designer
Duration
1 year
Team
Product Designer · Product Manager · Creative Director

Project overview

Brief

CLEA is an iOS-based, mobile corneal topographer that can assess dry eye, keratoconus, astigmatism, corneal power, and more — all from the palm of your hand.

Problem

Breaking into the well-established medical field with an iOS-based medical device is likely to be met with hesitation.

Solution

Mix traditional medical language with modern, user-friendly design so the device feels familiar to industry professionals while remaining accessible to all.

Understanding the user

Secondary research

To understand how users experience exit and cancellation flows, I explored existing patterns across apps and medical devices before proposing any design direction.

  • Exploration of cancellation and "are you sure" modals across mobile apps
  • Exploration of words like "cancel," "exit," "discard," and "quit" and how users interpret each
  • Comparison to other medical devices and the language conventions they use
  • Understanding how and why doctors might leave an exam mid-session, and what they expect to happen to their progress

Refining the design

Solution

My suggested solution is to use "exit" as the copy for leaving the exam and any progress made. It is apparent through the primary research that all 3 users preferred "exit" as the copy choice even when it was not an option. All 3 users separately suggested "exit," stating that it made the most sense in this situation.

The majority of users felt that having a label made exiting the exam easier and clearer, but that if a label was not to be used, then emphasizing the "X" was necessary to make it stand out more. A great next step would be to test "exit" against "discard" or other word choices to reaffirm it being the best choice or not. However, for now, I can confidently say that it is the best copy choice for this use case.

Finalizing the design

Optimized solution

Without testing more users, we can conclude that "exit" is the best choice for this copy. The word is clear, action-oriented, and aligns with the mental models of medical professionals accustomed to traditional device interfaces.

Conclusion

Results

Takeaways

Working on CLEA reinforced how critical precise, familiar language is in high-stakes environments. When the stakes are a patient's eye health, even small content decisions have real consequences for how professionals trust and use a product. I look forward to seeing the impact CLEA has on eye care professionals and patients around the world.
