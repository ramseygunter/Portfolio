Project overview

Brief

OnFleek Travel & Beyond is a startup company based in Seville, Spain. They offer trips throughout Spain, Portugal, and Morocco and are looking to expand their target audience to English-speaking study-abroad students currently living in Europe. My role as UX Content Writer was to empathize with the target audience to help design this trip to students' preferences and create content to bring attention to the company's unique opportunities.

Project

I used user-centered design thinking to design a trip that reflected study abroad students' needs, wants, and desires. Once the trip was designed, the trip description would be embellished with a friendly, upbeat, and casual voice to be cohesive with the OnFleek brand while still providing informative details and some of the historical aspects of the cities visited.

Problem

Study-abroad students living in Europe want to improve their language skills and cultural knowledge while doing fun activities to enhance their overall study-abroad experience.

Solution

Create and market an educational yet fun weekend trip to Lisbon, Sintra, and Cascais, allowing students to enhance their study abroad experience and enrich their studies.

Learning opportunity

Given that I was the only native English speaker on the team, I was responsible for user research and content writing. Not only was this my first time leading a design project, but it was also my first time working with a team solely in my second language, Spanish. Therefore, I prepared myself to translate my findings and design ideas into Spanish to best communicate with my team.

Understanding the user

I conducted interviews with people who were currently studying abroad or had studied abroad to truly understand the needs, behaviors, and motivations of the users. Then I used the qualitative data to create personas that reflected the users.

Designing the experience

Tour guides

While the OnFleek team worked in the office during the week, we all doubled as tour guides on the weekends. Upon reviewing and analyzing the user research, we discussed ways to provide students with the best experience possible. Working as a team, we devised a plan to ensure high customer satisfaction and to address the pain point of students not wanting "boring tour guides."

Itinerary rough draft
Planning

Once the itinerary rough draft was written, we worked as a team to find the best accommodations, local restaurants, and Portuguese tour guides to provide city highlights and history. We also ensured a balance between having the trip structured enough that students did not have to stress about planning, but also not too structured to allow plenty of free time for students to explore the cities on their own or with the OnFleek guides.
